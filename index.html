What is a Percentage? Understanding the Basics

The word "percentage" comes from the Latin "per centum," meaning "by the hundred." A percentage is a way to express a number as a fraction of 100. It's represented by the percent sign (%). When we say 50%, we mean 50 out of 100, or 50/100, which simplifies to ½.

Where We Use Percentages in Daily Life

  • Shopping: Sales and discounts in stores
  • Finance: Interest rates on loans and savings accounts
  • Technology: Battery charge indicators on devices
  • Nutrition: Information on food packaging
  • Education: Test scores and academic grades

How to Calculate Percentage: The Fundamental Formula

The basic percentage formula is essential for all percentage calculations:

Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100

Where:

  • Part: The portion of the whole you're interested in
  • Whole: The total or complete amount
  • Multiply by 100: Converts the decimal to a percentage

Example: Calculating a Test Score Percentage

If you answered 45 questions correctly out of 60 on a test, here's how to calculate your percentage score:

Percentage = (45 / 60) × 100 = 0.75 × 100 = 75%

3 Main Types of Percentage Calculations

1. How to Find the Percentage of a Number

Use this when you want to know what a certain percentage is of a given number.

Result = (Percentage / 100) × Number

Example: Calculating a Discount Amount

A $200 jacket is on sale for 25% off. What's the discount amount?

Discount = (25 / 100) × 200 = 0.25 × 200 = $50

Sale price = $200 - $50 = $150

2. How to Find What Percentage One Number Is of Another

This is the standard percentage calculation we saw earlier.

Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100

Example: Calculating Battery Percentage

Your phone shows 3,450 mAh remaining out of a 4,000 mAh battery. What percentage remains?

Percentage = (3,450 / 4,000) × 100 = 0.8625 × 100 = 86.25%

3. How to Find the Whole When the Percentage Is Known

Useful when you know a part and what percentage it represents of the whole.

Whole = Part / (Percentage / 100)

Example: Determining Total Sales from Commission

A salesperson made $8,400 in commission, which is 7% of their total sales. What were their total sales?

Total sales = 8,400 / (7 / 100) = 8,400 / 0.07 = $120,000

Step-by-Step: How to Calculate Percentages

  1. Identify the part and the whole: Determine which number represents the portion and which represents the total.
  2. Divide the part by the whole: This gives you a decimal number between 0 and 1.
  3. Multiply by 100: Convert the decimal to a percentage by moving the decimal point two places to the right.
  4. Add the percent sign: Include the % symbol to indicate it's a percentage.

How to Calculate Percentage Increase and Decrease

The formula for percentage change is:

Percentage Change = [(New Value - Old Value) / Old Value] × 100

Example: Calculating Price Increase Percentage

A product's price increased from $80 to $100. What's the percentage increase?

Percentage Increase = [(100 - 80) / 80] × 100 = (20 / 80) × 100 = 0.25 × 100 = 25%

Example: Calculating Population Decrease Percentage

A town's population decreased from 12,000 to 11,400. What's the percentage decrease?

Percentage Decrease = [(11,400 - 12,000) / 12,000] × 100 = (-600 / 12,000) × 100 = -0.05 × 100 = -5% (or 5% decrease)

Practical Applications: How to Use Percentage Calculations

1. Financial Percentage Calculations

Essential for:

  • Calculating interest rates on loans and savings
  • Determining investment returns
  • Understanding mortgage payments
  • Credit card APR calculations

Example: Calculating Simple Interest

You deposit $5,000 in a savings account with a 3% annual interest rate. How much interest will you earn in one year?

Interest = (3 / 100) × 5,000 = 0.03 × 5,000 = $150

2. Shopping Discount Percentage Calculations

Helps with:

  • Calculating sale prices
  • Comparing discounts between stores
  • Determining final prices after multiple markdowns

Example: Calculating Stacked Discounts

An item originally priced at $200 has a 20% discount, plus an additional 15% off the sale price. What's the final price?

First discount: (20 / 100) × 200 = $40 → Sale price = $160

Second discount: (15 / 100) × 160 = $24 → Final price = $136

Tips for Solving Percentage Problems

  1. Identify what represents 100%: This is your baseline for comparison.
  2. Convert percentages to decimals when multiplying: Move the decimal point two places left (25% becomes 0.25).
  3. Convert decimals to percentages when presenting results: Move the decimal point two places right (0.35 becomes 35%).
  4. Use the percentage formula triangle: Cover the variable you want to find to see which operation to perform.
  5. Check your answers: Does the result make sense in context?

Common Mistakes When Calculating Percentages

Watch out for these common errors:

  • Confusing the part and the whole: Always double-check which number represents the total.
  • Forgetting to multiply by 100: This leaves you with a decimal instead of a percentage.
  • Misplacing decimal points: Especially when converting between percentages and decimals.
  • Adding percentages of different wholes: Percentages can't be added unless they relate to the same base amount.

Advanced Percentage Calculations

1. Compound Percentage Changes

When multiple percentage changes occur sequentially, they don't simply add up.

Example: Compound Interest Calculation

You invest $1,000 at 5% annual interest compounded yearly for 3 years.

Year 1: 1,000 × 1.05 = $1,050

Year 2: 1,050 × 1.05 = $1,102.50

Year 3: 1,102.50 × 1.05 = $1,157.63

The total growth is 15.76%, not 15%, due to compounding.

2. Reverse Percentage Calculations

Finding the original amount when you know the final amount and the percentage change.

Example: Finding Original Price After Discount

A shirt costs $56 after a 30% discount. What was the original price?

$56 represents 70% of the original price (100% - 30%)

Original price = 56 / 0.70 = $80

Frequently Asked Questions About How to Calculate Percentages

Q: How do I calculate 20% of a number?

A: Multiply the number by 0.20 (which is 20/100). For example, 20% of 150 is 150 × 0.20 = 30.

Q: How do I add a percentage to a number?

A: Multiply the number by (1 + percentage as a decimal). To add 15% to 200: 200 × 1.15 = 230.

Q: How do I subtract a percentage from a number?

A: Multiply the number by (1 - percentage as a decimal). To subtract 20% from 500: 500 × 0.80 = 400.

Q: Why do we multiply by 100 in percentage calculations?

A: Multiplying by 100 converts the decimal fraction to a percentage, which is easier to understand and compare (e.g., 0.25 becomes 25%).
